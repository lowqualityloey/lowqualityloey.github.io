Steps and strategies for my learning journey

7 September 2025
What is your long-term goal or career pathway?
My long-term goal is to work in a software development role that aligns with my passion, ideally in a hybrid working setup. I want to find a position that allows me to maintain a healthy work-life balance without constant stress over deadlines. If I settle comfortably into a role, I would like the flexibility to travel to places I’ve always wanted to explore while working remotely.
A description of your strengths and limitations when it comes to learning
I am highly focused and driven when I am engaged in work that interests me, especially when creativity and attention to detail are involved. However, I can get easily distracted or lose interest if I lose that focus or if the task becomes unenjoyable. Staying motivated requires that the work remains stimulating and meaningful for me.
What skills (non-technical/human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?
I want to build my confidence and improve my communication skills, as I sometimes feel hesitant to express my ideas and thoughts clearly. Additionally, I aim to develop stronger analytical skills and become more diligent and responsible in managing my work effectively.
A commitment to manage your workload in this programme
  • I manage my time using Toggl's Pomodoro technique, which helps me stay disciplined and focused by breaking work into intervals with regular breaks. I am conscious of respecting others around me, ensuring that I don’t disrupt fellow learners or facilitators while working.
  • When things start to build up and feel overwhelming, I plan to pause and take breaks to avoid burnout. If needed, I won’t hesitate to reach out to facilitators for support.
  • I commit to seeking help early whenever I face challenges, whether that means asking questions during sessions or approaching facilitators individually.
  • I expect the facilitation team to provide clear guidance, timely feedback, and a supportive environment that encourages questions and continuous learning. From my experience so far, they have been great, and I hope that continues.
  • I do not currently have scheduled block-out times for other commitments.
